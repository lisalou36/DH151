About Me

Hello to all reading this. My name is Lisa, and I am a second year MLIS student (that is, Masters of Library and Information Science). My specializations are Archival Science and Academic Librarianship. As an undergraduate student, I also went to UCLA and majored in History with a minor in Film, TV, and Digital Media. Because of some great internships I had as an undergraduate at the Fox Studios Archive (RIP) and the Center for the Study of Political Graphics (CSPG), I began to see how I could apply my boundless interest in History. Archival science and archives themselves focus on preserving the history of a community and making it accessible to that community. When I first entered the MLIS program, my sole interest was archives. However, after having worked at Powell for a year doing research and reference work and having been a TA for a History class, my interest in working in an academic library as an instructional librarian has caught up to my interest in pursuing archival work. It's a conundrum, considering I graduate in about 2 months... But I'd be happy going in either direction, so I'm trying not to stress (famous last words)!

Besides my career interests, things that make me happy include:

  • Painting and Sketching -- Except oil paint, I do NOT have the patience that oil painting requires
  • Big dogs, including horses
  • Thematic cookbooks -- especially the old ones, even though I'm not really a fan of cooking
  • Binging TV & Movies -- (anyone wanna talk about Falcon and the Winter Soldier? anyone NOT wanna talk about WW1984?)
  • Working at Trader Joe's -- If my whole ~work in a library or archive~ thing doesn't pan out, I'd be cool with staying at Trader Joe's

Goals for this Course

  • I have been really interested in becoming more technologically literate for the past several years, but I was always too intimidated to take a class, and when I tried teaching myself, I'd get stuck and quit. Tragic.
  • It sounds trite to say, but I just care a lot about a lot of global issues. I feel overwhelmed and cynical about the future, and being able to channel that frustration into productive projects is helpful to me, and hopefully others
  • I also recently got into maps.
    • I helped host an event Fall Quarter featuring LA Public Library's Map Librarian, and he was one of the coolest people ever to talk to.
    • Around the same time, the class I was TA'ing for had an assignment that required the students to use Stanford's David Rumsey Map Collection, and it was one of the most mindblowing adaptations of primary source materials to publicly available online content I've ever seen.
    • And now I'm really into maps. (So a goal is to help me become more acquainted with how historical maps can be used with modern pedagogical tools).
